Public health and ecomomic problems caused by storms and other severe weather events

Synopsis

The following analyses address two questions:

1. Across the United States, which types of events are most harmful with respect to population health?

2. Across the United States, which types of events have the greatest economic consequences?

The data is first processed to extract the variables of interest for this study, followed by a series of calculations for each event type. I present two graphs as well as full tables output at the end of the document. 

Results show that in general tornado is the main concern for public health and ecomomic problems. Injuries related to weather events are generally more numerous than fatalities, whereas properties suffer greater losses than crops.

Justification:
For Question 1, the first step is to decide which variables from the original dataset (which I loaded above, i.e. "df") qualify for "population health". After inspection of the variables description from the Storm Data  Documentation (https://d396qusza40orc.cloudfront.net/repdata%2Fpeer2_doc%2Fpd01016005curr.pdf), it appears that the columns "FATALITIES" and "INJURIES" report the number of event-related fatalities and injuries. 

For Question 2, I choose to define "economic consequences" as the losses in US dollars caused by the event type. Two columns qualify for economic losses in US dollars: "PROPDMG" and "CROPDMG", corresponding to property damage amount and crop damage amount, respectively.

The subsequent analyses for this project will therefore use the following columns from the original dataset:
- "EVTYPE": Event type
- "FATALITIES": Event-related fatalities
- "INJURIES": Event-related injuries
- "PROPDMG": Property damage (in US dallar)
- "CROPDMG": Crop damage (in US dallar)

Results:
The above plot (as well as the full tables below) indicate that tornado is the most harmful event to population health. The plot also indicates that injuries related to weather events are generally more numerous than fatalities. Other weather events that are harmful to population health include excessive heat, flood, lightning, and TSTM wind.
