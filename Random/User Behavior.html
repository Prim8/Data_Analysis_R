A study of web client login behavior

This project uses web client login time for the month of March 2012. The goal of this project is to explore the data set and generate graphs showing the breakdown of client login behavior. A short write up follows the results.

Part 2 - Breakdown the data set by day, week, and hour of the day

day <- logs$mday  # 1–31: day of the month (?POSIXlt)

week <- logs$wday  # 0–6 day of the week, starting on Sunday 

hour <- logs$hour  # 0–23: hours

Part 4 - Write up

It is clear from the graphs that there is a cyclical aspect to web client login, which peaked around the 20th of March. The breakdown of logins by day of the week shows more activity during the weekends and slower activity at the beginning of the week. Morevover, on a hourly basis, it appears that web client login behavior increases between roughly 8PM to 3AM, and peaks during late nights, more specifically between midnight and 1AM. On the other hand, there is a clear decrease in web logins between 6AM to 10AM. Client login behavior seems to be constant in the afternoons.

Given that weekends and evenings are the busiest web traffic times, it is important to maintain the quality of our services during those times and ensure that service is available to clients. It is equally important to investigate strategies that would help increase web client logins outside of the peak times as identified above as they may lead to potential transactions.
